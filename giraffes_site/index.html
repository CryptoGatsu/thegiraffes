Why Giraffes Endure Storms

Giraffes evolved to survive open plains where storms are unavoidable. Their height allows early awareness, their cardiovascular system handles pressure, and their instinct is calm observation rather than panic.

Why They Don’t Lay Down

Lying down makes giraffes vulnerable. Standing allows instant reaction. They conserve energy and remain ready — a rare survival strategy.

How Giraffes Sleep

Giraffes sleep as little as 20–30 minutes a day, often standing. Deep sleep is brief and risky, making them one of the lightest sleepers in nature.
