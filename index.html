Why Giraffes Endure Storms

Giraffes evolved to survive open plains where storms are unavoidable. Their height provides early awareness, their cardiovascular system handles extreme pressure, and their instinct is calm observation — not panic.

Why They Don’t Lay Down

Lying down makes giraffes vulnerable. Standing allows instant reaction. They conserve energy, remain alert, and avoid unnecessary risk.

How Giraffes Sleep

Giraffes sleep as little as 20–30 minutes per day, often standing. Deep sleep lasts only moments. They are built to never be caught off guard.
